    Celsius Conversion Formula

    Celsius to Fahrenheit °F = °C × 9/5 + 32
    Celsius to Kelvin K = °C + 273.15
    Celsius to Rankine °R = °C × 9/5 + 491.67
    Celsius to Réaumur °Ré = °C × 4/5

    💡 Do you know that?

    °C

    Celsius: The most commonly used temperature scale worldwide, with water freezing at 0°C and boiling at 100°C.

    °F

    Fahrenheit: Commonly used in the United States. Water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F.

    K

    Kelvin: The international (SI) unit of measurement for science. 0K is "absolute zero", where all molecules stop moving.

    °R

    Rankine: The absolute temperature scale based on Fahrenheit, commonly used in the field of thermodynamics in the United States.

    °Re

    Réaumur: An old temperature scale from France. Water freezes at 0°Re and boils at 80°Re.
